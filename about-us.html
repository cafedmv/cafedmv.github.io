About Us

Community Art For Everyone is a 501(c)(3), non-profit organization. We aim to make cities safer and more beautiful through community art activities. By embracing our community's diverse culture, we hope to bring vitality and happiness to us all.

Learn More


What is Community Art, and why does it matter?


‘Community Art’ is a new form of public art that allows members of a community to participate in art creation directly or collaboratively with artists. Through this, individuals are able to share and express their feelings outwardly in new and empowering ways.

Recently, community art has used as a means of solving urban and social problems. Our mission is to create a healthier and more attractive DC Metro area through cultural and social rehabilitation of the urban regeneration project which is being performed in cities around the world.

Our Founder, Hannah Joo


Hannah is interested in community art as a combination of city, humanity, and art. Hannah’s experiences extend that of art exhibition, community art, and art education. She has worked as a curator at Gail Art Museum in South Korea where she developed exhibitions for and the education of contemporary art, public art, and community engaged art, and also children’s projects. In addition to curating exhibitions Hannah created the Disabled Community Exhibition with 150 disabled artists, also the Young Art Festival with 230 young artists, and the Youth Art Festival with 600 high school students. She was an instructor at Yeongdong University in Korea and lectured on community culture, as well as lecturing to several professionals such as public school teachers, social workers and government officers.
