About Us

Community Art For Everyone is a 501(c)(3), non-profit organization fueled by an endless passion to do good for our world one brush stroke at a time. We work closely with local government, companies and neighborhoods to bring happiness to all of us here in the DMV.

Who We Are



What is CAFE, and how do we help the DMV?


We are Community Art For Everyone, or "CAFE" for short. ‘Community Art’ is a new form of public art that allows members of a community to participate in art creation directly or collaboratively with artists. Through this, individuals are able to share and express their feelings outwardly in new and empowering ways.

Recently, community art has used as a means of solving urban and social problems. Our mission is to create a healthier and more attractive DC Metro area through cultural and social rehabilitation of the urban regeneration project which is being performed in cities around the world.

Our Founder, Hannah Joo


Hannah is interested in community art as a way of blending cities, people and art together. Her professional background extends to art exhibitions, community art projects, and art education. She has worked as a curator at Gail Art Museum in South Korea where she developed exhibitions to educate the public on contemporary, public and community engaged art. She also designed and facilitated a series of children’s projects.

In addition to curating exhibitions, Hannah also created the Disabled Community Exhibition which featured over 150 disabled artists, the Young Art Festival which featured over 230 young artists and the Youth Art Festival with over 600 high school participants.

As an instructor at Yeongdong University in Korea, she lectured on community culture and gave lectures to professionals from public school teachers to social workers and government officers.
